The project details

To understand this project you have to learn more about the start-up which hired me, Collock.
It sells Team Building services to companies. Their specialty being the Escape Game, they chose this time to do it in the form of a video game for their new big customer: Enedis.
So I had the chance to work with their designer and the project manager by fulfilling the role of Unity developer.

The concept of the game had been closely built with the sponsors of Enedis, who expected a very precise result bringing many constraints on the technical aspect that the puzzles had to have. The players follow a line that takes place in a future where an electrical failure occurs on a lunar colony. The goal is therefore to solve a series of problems in the form of puzzles and mini games in order to help the inhabitants of the colony.
One of the central elements of the gameplay is teamwork, so the game is multiplayer and from this comes several constraints that the customer had set.

  • The game had to be able to run in a web browser.
    Indeed, I had to provide a version of the game playable in browser in order to allow access to the game without any download.

  • It was mandatory that the whole server part was done on a server that Collock already had.
    This was a major challenge for me because I had never really performed such a complex task in this area of expertise because it involved making the game accessible on the Enedis intranet, which is a closed network. So I found myself having to train on the subject and working with network administrators at Enedis when I had almost no knowledge in networking.


    • These two constraints made the operation extremely technical, especially since the restrictions on their firewalls were not communicated to me for safety reasons, so I was working in a blind manner. However, it was very rewarding and very rewarding so I do not regret these almost 6 months of missions.
